# JRAG Audience Landing Pages

The `Professionnels` and `Particuliers` landing pages are intentionally asymmetric. Both follow a six-section pattern, but the B2B journey prioritizes evaluation and lead conversion toward `Contact Pro`, while the B2C journey prioritizes shopping entry and uses reassurance, inspiration, and account value as conversion support around `Boutique`.

## Professionnels

### Hero

- Purpose: Communicate the B2B supply proposition for restaurants, hotels, wholesalers, and export buyers, and establish the `Professionnels` journey immediately.
- Must-have content: Value proposition for professional seafood supply; audience signals for restaurants, hotels, wholesalers, and export; concrete quality and origin cues such as sourcing region, handling standards, or species range; direct route toward evaluation or contact.
- CTA behavior: Primary CTA is `Contact Pro`; secondary CTA routes to `Produits` or to a visit or sample request if that option is surfaced.

### Offer overview

- Purpose: Clarify the professional offer quickly so buyers understand product families, available formats, and commercial relevance.
- Must-have content: Product families; format and packaging cues; offer clarity by use case or buyer type; link into deeper product detail.
- CTA behavior: Primary CTA goes to `Produits`; secondary CTA can jump to the most relevant offer family or format detail.

### Savoir-faire

- Purpose: Build operational trust by showing sourcing, handling, purification, and traceability capabilities.
- Must-have content: Sourcing approach; handling standards; purification process; traceability proof.
- CTA behavior: Primary CTA goes to the dedicated `Savoir-faire` page; secondary CTA goes to `Contact Pro` for buyers ready to qualify the offer.

### Proof

- Purpose: Validate buyer confidence with certifications, sectors served, and references or client-type proof.
- Must-have content: Certification highlights; sectors served; reference examples or client types; clear explanation of why each proof point matters for professional evaluation.
- CTA behavior: Primary CTA goes to `Certifications` or `Secteurs / Clients`; secondary CTA goes to `Contact Pro` once enough proof has been established.

### Operational reassurance

- Purpose: Reduce hesitation around logistics, consistency, and the quality process before a buyer reaches out.
- Must-have content: Logistics or delivery support notes; consistency and supply reliability cues; quality process summary; clear explanation of supply continuity, response expectations, or account handling for ongoing orders.
- CTA behavior: Primary CTA is a quote request or direct `Contact Pro`; secondary CTA can offer a visit request where relevant.

### Conversion CTA block

- Purpose: Convert qualified interest into a clear B2B action at the end of the landing page.
- Must-have content: Quote request option; visit request option; test-product option; direct professional contact path.
- CTA behavior: Present `Contact Pro` or quote request as the primary CTA, with visit request and product testing as clearly secondary actions.

## Particuliers

### Hero

- Purpose: Present the premium seafood proposition for consumers and establish the `Particuliers` journey immediately.
- Must-have content: Consumer-facing premium seafood proposition; clear freshness and quality cues such as origin, preparation, or product standard; lifestyle or enjoyment cue; clear route into shopping.
- CTA behavior: Primary CTA is `Boutique`; secondary CTA can route to featured categories or inspiration content.

### Shop entry

- Purpose: Move consumers quickly into product discovery through featured categories and a clear route into the boutique.
- Must-have content: Featured categories; boutique entry point; product discovery shortcuts; category cues that simplify shopping.
- CTA behavior: Primary CTA goes to `Boutique`; secondary CTA can open a featured category directly.

### Reassurance

- Purpose: Reduce friction around purchase by reinforcing freshness, delivery, payment, and service trust.
- Must-have content: Freshness reassurance; delivery expectations; payment trust signals; clear customer-service cues such as support availability, delivery help, or issue resolution guidance.
- CTA behavior: Primary CTA returns users to `Boutique`; secondary CTA can point to delivery or checkout-support information.

### Inspiration

- Purpose: Support discovery and desire through recipes and usage ideas that connect content to products.
- Must-have content: Recipe highlights; usage ideas; product pairings or links; inspiration entry into editorial content.
- CTA behavior: Primary CTA goes to `Recettes & Conseils`; secondary CTA links back into relevant product categories.

### Loyalty / account value

- Purpose: Increase repeat-purchase intent by explaining account and loyalty benefits; this is supporting conversion content rather than the main purchase entry.
- Must-have content: Repeat-purchase benefits; account value explanation; loyalty or membership advantages; reason to create an account after or alongside first purchase.
- CTA behavior: Primary CTA encourages account creation through `Mon Compte`; secondary CTA highlights loyalty-supported return shopping.

### Conversion CTA block

- Purpose: End the consumer journey with a strong purchase or account action.
- Must-have content: Shop-now option; create-account option; discover-categories option; concise purchase-oriented reassurance.
- CTA behavior: Primary CTA is `Boutique`; secondary CTAs support account creation or category discovery.
