# JRAG Agent Page Intent Map

Use this file to understand what each page family is trying to do.

## Homepage

Role: brand router.

Must do:

- establish JRAG's world
- create trust
- show the B2B/B2C split clearly
- route users into the right path

It should not become a full brochure for every audience at once.

## Professionnels

Role: luxury supplier journey.

Must feel:

- rigorous
- high-trust
- operational
- premium

Primary outcome: push toward `Contact Pro` or deeper evaluation.

## Particuliers

Role: luxury consumer journey.

Must feel:

- desirable
- reassuring
- premium
- easy to shop from

Primary outcome: push toward `Boutique` and discovery.

## A propos and Story Pages

Role: brand publishing and identity.

Must feel:

- more editorial
- more spacious
- more atmospheric

These pages carry the origin, terroir, narrative, credibility, and long-term brand value.

## Contact Pages

Role: conversion with reassurance.

Must feel:

- clear
- premium
- direct
- trustworthy

Do not make contact pages feel like plain utility forms.

## B2B Proof Pages

Examples:

- `Produits`
- `Savoir-faire`
- `Certifications`
- `Secteurs / Clients`

These pages must convert proof into buyer confidence.
They should be structured, legible, and commercial.

## B2C Support Pages

Examples:

- `Boutique`
- `Recettes & Conseils`
- `Livraison`
- `Programme Fidelite`

These pages should support desire, reassurance, and shopping flow.

## Resources Pages

Examples:

- `Ressources`
- `Actualites`
- `FAQ`
- `Galerie`

These pages should feel curated, useful, and connected to the brand world.

## Utility Pages

Examples:

- `Mon Compte`
- `Panier`
- `Paiement`

These pages should stay in-brand, but function comes first.
Use typography, spacing, and polish to keep them aligned with the rest of the site.

## Legal Pages

Examples:

- `Mentions legales`
- `Confidentialite`
- `CGV B2B`
- `CGV B2C`
- `Plan du site`

These pages should be the calmest version of the system.
No drama needed, but they should still feel like JRAG.

## Final Rule for Agents

When working on any page, define these three things before designing:

1. What is the page trying to make the user feel?
2. What is the page trying to make the user trust?
3. What is the page trying to make the user do next?

If those are unclear, the page will drift.
