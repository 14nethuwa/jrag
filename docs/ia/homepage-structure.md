# JRAG Homepage Structure

## Hero

- Purpose: establish the brand proposition and present two audience CTAs
- Target audience impact: Gives both professional buyers and consumers an immediate understanding of JRAG, clarifies that the homepage is a router rather than a full journey, and makes the first-click decision obvious.
- Primary CTA or interaction: Two equally prominent primary CTAs, one to `Professionnels` and one to `Particuliers`, supported by a short headline, a concise supporting line, and only light secondary content.

## Why JRAG

- Purpose: build trust around quality, origin, and expertise
- Target audience impact: Reassures both audiences that JRAG is credible, premium, and operationally reliable before they commit to a deeper B2B or B2C path.
- Primary CTA or interaction: Present a compact trust/value block, ideally 3 short pillars or proof statements, with an optional secondary link into `A propos` or origin/story content rather than a large standalone CTA.

## Featured products / categories

- Purpose: create product interest without replacing the full catalog
- Target audience impact: Builds curiosity around the offer while keeping users focused on the right next journey instead of turning the homepage into a browsing-heavy catalog page.
- Primary CTA or interaction: Surface a small set of featured product families or categories with direct links into the relevant audience destination, keeping this section teaser-level rather than exhaustive.

## Terroir / Storytelling

- Purpose: reinforce origin and emotional brand identity
- Target audience impact: Deepens emotional trust and premium differentiation for both professional and consumer audiences by connecting products to place, sourcing, and brand identity.
- Primary CTA or interaction: Use one clear route into story-led content such as `Terroir Dakhla` or `Notre histoire`, supported by a brief origin narrative and a visual or proof cue; keep this tighter than a full `A propos` page.

## Choose your path

- Purpose: re-offer the B2B/B2C split for undecided users
- Target audience impact: Helps undecided visitors self-select into the right journey after seeing brand and product context, especially if they skipped or hesitated at the hero.
- Primary CTA or interaction: Re-present two explicit audience cards or buttons for `Professionnels` and `Particuliers`, each with a short descriptor so the user can identify the correct path quickly.

## Trust signals

- Purpose: surface certifications, quality proof, clients, or reassurance
- Target audience impact: Reduces hesitation by showing concise proof points relevant to supplier evaluation, purchase confidence, and overall brand legitimacy.
- Primary CTA or interaction: Show a lean proof module with items such as certifications, client/reference cues, quality handling proof, or delivery reassurance, with an optional link to deeper proof pages like `Certifications` instead of multiple competing actions.

## Editorial discovery

- Purpose: expose recipes, blog, or featured content
- Target audience impact: Encourages deeper exploration, repeat visits, and product-use inspiration beyond the main routing flow without distracting from audience routing.
- Primary CTA or interaction: Feature a small set of editorial entries, such as recipes, blog posts, or a highlighted article, with one clear discovery route into `Recettes & Conseils` or `Actualites / Blog`; keep this section curated rather than content-dense.

## Footer

- Purpose: expose the full shared architecture and legal links
- Target audience impact: Gives all users a final navigation safety net across shared pages, audience areas, utilities, and legal destinations, especially when they do not convert from the homepage body.
- Primary CTA or interaction: Use grouped footer navigation that clearly separates shared, audience, utility, and legal links; completeness is core here, while promotional content can stay lean.
